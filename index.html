Kayaking at Loch Lomond

Kayaking is my favourite pastime because it can be so peaceful and calm out on the waters, with quiet shorelines and wide open breath taking views. But conditions can change at any moment and become very challenging - that mix makes it fun and rewarding for me. At Loch Lomond, there are many places to explore: islands, bays, and long stretches of water perfect for gentle paddling or stronger workouts.

If you're new, Loch Lomond Leisure is a local provider that offers kayak hire and (seasonal) guided sessions, plus safety gear like buoyancy aids. You can look them up and book ahead, especially in summer. Always check the weather, dress for getting wet, and tell someone your plan.

Why I love it

  1. Peaceful escapes with amazing views
  2. Can quickly turn into a fun challenge
  3. Great exercise and very rewarding

Simple starter tips

  1. Wear a buoyancy aid and check the weather.
  2. Start near the shore and build cofidence.
  3. Bring water, a snack, and a dry change of clothes.

Remember: conditions on the loch can change quickly. Plan your route, carry a phone in a dry bag, and be prepared to turn back if the wind picks up.
